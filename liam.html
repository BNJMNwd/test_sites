Meet Liam

Liam is a three-year-old male ring-tailed lemur, a.k.a. "Boss." He is my personal pet and sleeps in the house with me, though he feels it is his job to protect not only me, but the other animals, as well. He loves them all and does NOT take kindly to other humans coming in to pet ANYONE!
Unfortunately, he will be caged during guest visits, though he is fun to watch in the videos.

Quick facts about ring-Tailed Lemurs

  • Native to Madagascar and some tiny neighboring islands
  • Have a long, boldly striped, black-and-white tail that can't be used for gripping
  • Diet consists of mostly fruit, but also eat insects, leaves, flowers, tree bark and sap
  • From the genus Lemur
  • Have powerful scent glands to mark their territory and communicate with others
  • Live in-group’s known as troops, which include 6-30 animals but average 17
  • Endangered because their habitat of sparse, dry forests is vanishing
  • Average life span in the wild is 18 years
  • Adults weigh an average of 4.9 lbs. with a body length between 15 and 18 inches, not including its tail, which is between 22 and 25 inches
